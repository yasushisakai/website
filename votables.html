Votables

For ornot.vote, I just started to use one ’string’ to represent a plan, because I knew this is a complicated topic how to define what a vote is or a ’votable’ is, even when mechanically writing traits.

I can imagine this topic going all over the place, from technical implementation to philosophical statements.

This is a memo how to start thinking about different votes and voters. Perhaps I name this abstract concept a ’Votable’: Something that can and could be voted to.

’Votables’ can vote for….

1 itself

It’s a somewhat non intuitive from the beginning, but this is important. To get down the point, in order to capture the overall points to a plan, the plan needs to vote for solely itself. By making it vote for it selves, from the voting Matrix point of view, this creates a drain to suck every vote that was cast into.

In contrast, the discussion of whether people should be able to vote for themselves is another thing. The general fact is to avoid every one casting their whole vote to themselves. If this happens, together with the fact that every plan votes for themselves, the result of the poll will be undefined.

This gets increasingly unlikely if we have more participants, since the dimension of the matrix will follow. (really?)

For the system, I’m thinking to apply the ’one can not vote to him/herself’ rule, in favor of the stable output. This is because this tool is to help building consensus, but it’s worth putting it in words that this is a deliberate design decision on purpose. This might change.

2 a plan.

In simple terms one can image ’plan’s like ’ramen’, ’kung pao chiken’, ’pizza’, ’sushi’. When you want to decide what to eat.

Yet these things could have different abstractness.

Some options could be generalized (categorized), like ’ramen’ and ’sushi’ can be generalized to ’Japanese’. Japanese could be generalized to Asian, with the combination of ’Chinese’.

But wait, there is even more abstractness… why are we deciding on what to eat, when did we build consensus on this. Isn’t considering how to save earth is the absolute top priority? Just for a simple ’text’ based option, if you think about generalization and categorization or in the terms of mathematical sets, it’s interesting how abstract a ’votable’ thing is..

Another discussion is the interface of the votes. Form the time being, plans are only expressive in the form of words, and the whole motivation to write this memo was to think about what other form should a plan take. A simple word like ’ramen’ is a pointer to the actual dish with hot animal broth with noodles. So even a ’word’ plan is a link. In the era of all information connected together, via the www, this usually means that any form of link could work. Like an url. This could also be a image. Here is other things I think it should be considered as plans.

  • url
  • image
  • geolocation
    • point
    • geofences

Putting in ’smart contracts’ in the loop, a plan could be a function written in machine language, or in other words, an algorithm. Yet, this starts to look like a very different thing then simple options like ’ramen’ or an image of an fancy architectural rendering. I think is safe to put algorithms in the next category.

3 then comes the indirect component - we humans?

Similar to algorithms we humans can be also a generalized bag of plans, we want to treat it so much different compared to inanimate ’plans’ like ramen.

but more I develop it and feel the flow of the votes trying to converge, the difference is very subtle. Only one principal that a human can vote to any other plan or voter. As we thought about defining a plans is, some plans are relatively abstract then others. (Like japanese, ramen) these can vote for other votes, not to humans.

The difference in abstractness within these dynamic voters is none. Meaning, if we have a group of people, for example a Senete, it has the same function as one individual voter, expect that it is an artificial group that consists by people assumed that we (or it) has more aggregated power.

4 Vote through time

Not voting is widely considered unethical and I heard it is subject to prosecution in some places. But this can mean a lot of things. It could come from pure laziness. It could come from lacking confidence that the voter is not ready to decide given the amount of considerations that needs to take place, or positively endorsing other ’votables’, declaring I voting for what ever the future result is. But if you can vote for the present or the future, there might be options to vote for the past. So a there might be a ’votable’ that points to the past.

5 Generalization

so how to distinguish types of votes is a deep path down the road. The most generalized definition I think is, a ’votable’ is an actor that votes for any number of other voters. I feel like [needs citation] it’s close to the definition of Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz, ’monad’ where the most smallest unit of the universe is this abstract entity that reflects it self.

But this isn’t too much useful when you want to make structures when you are programming.

a poll is an result from a different time zone.

Another difference is atomic plans are static, they do not change their voting behavior. Hyper plans are deterministic. Once there is a clear consensus that Ramen is a type of Japanese dish, that categorization doesn’t change too much often. Yet there will be some subtleties here, since Ramen might be considered Italian in the future. But for the sake of developing I think it’s safe to assume that once an atomic plan is thought to be inside a hyper plan, it won’t change within a single voting process. But the other way around is different, as atomic plans are added, the set of that hyper plan will alter. This structured feature is shared with organizations and polls. The behavior of voting is predictable. People’s voting behavior is the most non deterministic. Essentially they are the ones to add other modes of ’vot-ables’ including other people and categories.

(static) atomic plan > (deterministic) > hyper plans, polls, organization > (non-deterministic) people
